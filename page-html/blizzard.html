Blizzard

What is it ?

Blizzard is a big snowstorm. It is essential that the air be very cold as it

has to be below freezing for snow to fall and it is required a lot of moisture

in the air for the snowflakes to form.

How is it formed ?

Blizzard starts in the same way as another storms. When two different massive

portions of air colide, cold and warm air, the warmed is pushed up and the

cold stays below the warm air. This makes a perfect condition for wind

and water coming together. As the water goes down, it gets freezed by the

cold air and snow emerge. In order to be considered a blizzard, three things must happen:

- Winds with at least 55km/h

- Low visibility

- It has to last 3 or more hours long

Consequences

This phenomenon can leave behing a massive quantity of snow accumulated.

The main consequences are power outage, canceled flights and heavy traffic

and congestion as it becomes impossible or hard enough to go anywhere.

As a curiosity, Snowstorms are one type of winter storm. Icy winter storms

bring freezing rain or sleet as well as snow. Blizzards are snowstorms with high winds.
