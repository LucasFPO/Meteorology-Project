Drought

What is it ?

Drought is a prolonged period of high temperatures and lack of precipitation.

A drought can be related to an overuse of water supply and overpopulation

as well. When too much people use and demand water and less than

average rainfall occurs to regenerate.

How is it formed ?

This phenomenon occurs when persistent high pressure in the atmosphere

curbs cloud formation due to lower evaporation. That leads to lower relative

humidity in atmosphere and hence, less precipitation. The high pressure system, when bigger,

is capable to persist for months or more, blocking storms carrying rain and snow.

Consequences

The devastation of a drought can be enormous as it destroy the crop making

less food available, it dries out the water from reservoirs reducing the

amount of potable water available and It can be dangerous to human health

as well because the air quality becomes low and particles from wildfires

and dry soils can irritate the airways and worsen chronic respiratory illnesses

and increase respiratory infections.
