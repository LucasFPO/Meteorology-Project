Hurricane

What is it ?

Hurricanes are in fact big powerful storms. It is characterized as a region

with significantly low pressure system and with temperatures higher than

its surroundings. As a curiosity, Hurricane can have other names such as Typhoon or Cyclone.

How is it formed ?

In order to form, a hurricane need a combination of different things such as:

- warm ocean water, at least 27Â°C, in order to make evaporation favorable, that later is gonna originate clouds.

- High leves of moisture in the atmosphere.

- Calm and steady wind, as it do not push away the clouds, making favorable the emergence of storms.

- The storm tends to get bigger and bigger as it is nourished by the elements above.

- The storm begins to spin around itself and move. As long as it has the necessary fuel, it keep growing and growing and with that a hurricane is formed.

Hurricanes die down when they move over land or out of the tropics and into cooler latitudes.

Consequences

As it is so powerful, A hurricane can bring devastation to coastal areas.

When reach land, the phenomenon bring with itself massive waves, tremendous

amount of rain in a short amount of time, flood, power outage and billions

in damage. They are the most powerful of all weather systems. In the North Atlantic,

hurricane season is from June 1st to November 30th each year.
