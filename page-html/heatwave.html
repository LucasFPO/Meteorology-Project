Heat Wave

What is it ?

A heatwave refers to a prolonged period of hot weather, which may be

accompanied by high humidity. It is a unusual hot weather over a region

persisting at least two consecutive days.

How is it formed ?

Heat waves are generally the result of trapped air. High-pressure systems

force air downward. This force prevents air near the ground from rising.

The sinking air acts like a cap. It traps warm ground air in place. Without

rising air, there are no rain, and nothing to prevent the hot air from get hotter.

Consequences

This phenomenon can be dangerous as is related to be cause of many deaths.

Elderly and kids are more susceptible to heat wave impacts. It is important

to keep yourself protected and hydrated.

If the hot weather is too prolonged, plants will struggle to find enough water,

go to seed and it will have a knock on effect on insects. Animals will struggle

as well, as they are desperate for water and it can cause effects on breeds and reproduction.
