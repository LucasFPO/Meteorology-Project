What is it ?

Considering that air pressure is the amount of force the molecules exert

on a given area; wind is caused by air flowing from an area of high pressure,

strong molecules cohesion, to an area of low pressure, weak molecules cohesion.

How is it formed ?

Air flows from high-pressure to low-pressure system to try to reach equilibrium

and by doing that, wind is created.

Bigger the difference between high and low pressure, stronger is the wind.

The Coriolis Effect that describes the flow of air masses says that the

rotation of Earth deflects the direction of the winds to the right in

Northern Hemisphere and left in Southern Hemisphere.

Consequences

Wind can be sometimes just a breeze or strong enough to blow the roofs

off buildings. This phenomenon can be as well an effective erosion and

transportation agent creating, for example, massive dunes. An incredible

benefit from wind is the possibility to convert the air motion kinetic

energy, wind power, in electricity. The so called Eolic Energy.
