Cold Wave

What is it ?

A cold wave is a weather phenomenon that is distinguished by a cooling

of the air. It is a rapid fall in temperature within a 24h period.

A cold wave can be also called as a cold snap.

How is it formed ?

A cold wave is determined by the rate at wich the temperature falls and

the minimum to which it falls this minimum temperature is dependent on the

geographical region and time of the year

Consequences

This phenomenon demands protection to agriculture industry, commerce

and social activities as a cold wave can cause death and injure to crops,

livestock, wildlife and people. As the temperatures fall, the risk of

hypothermia increases. Diseases like cold flu and pneumonia becomes

commom when in a cold snap.
